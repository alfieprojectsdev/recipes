# Family Recipes (Scaled for 5)

### **1. Lugaw (Instant Pot / Stovetop)**
*Yield: 5-6 Servings | Prep time: 10 mins | Cook time: 25 mins*

## ingredients
* 3 cups rice (Jasmine or Glutinous)
* 1 kg Chicken quarters (rubbed with ginger/salt)
* 2 chicken bouillon cubes
* 3-inch ginger (sliced into strips)
* 1 whole head garlic (minced/fried)
* Water (to "Max" line for IP, or 1:7 ratio for stovetop)

## instructions
1. **IP:** Saute garlic, ginger, and chicken. Add rice and water. Set to High Pressure for 20 mins. Natural release.
2. **Stovetop:** Boil chicken and ginger until tender. Add rice; stir frequently until grains bloom and texture is thick.
3. Season with salt or fish sauce to taste.

## additional notes / alternative ingredients
* **Toppings:** Hard-boiled eggs (1 per person), calamansi, and extra fried garlic.
* **Sub:** Use a mix of 2 cups regular rice and 1 cup malagkit for better texture.

### **2. Max’s Style Fried Chicken**
*Yield: 5 Servings | Prep time: 60 mins | Cook time: 60 mins*

## ingredients
* 1.5 kg Chicken (roughly 1.5 whole chickens or 8-10 leg quarters)
* Calamansi juice (1/3 cup)
* 3 tbsp Fish sauce (patis)
* 1.5 tsp Black pepper
* Oil for deep frying

## instructions
1. Marinate chicken in calamansi, patis, and pepper for at least 1 hour.
2. Steam the chicken for 30–45 minutes until fully cooked.
3. Remove from steamer and let it air-dry completely (surface must be bone-dry for the "crackle").
4. Flash-fry in very hot oil (180°C) until skin is golden brown and crisp.

## additional notes / alternative ingredients
* **Note:** You will likely need to fry in batches to maintain oil temperature.

### **3. Chicken Pastel**
*Yield: 5-6 Servings | Prep time: 15 mins | Cook time: 25 mins*

## ingredients
* 800g - 1kg Chicken breast or thighs (cubed)
* 1.5 cups heavy cream or 1 large can evaporated milk
* 2 Potatoes & 2 Carrots (cubed)
* 1 large can Mushrooms (sliced)
* Butter, garlic, and onions
* 1 cup Chorizo de Bilbao or hotdog (sliced)

## instructions
1. Sear chicken in butter until white; set aside.
2. Saute garlic, onion, chorizo/hotdog, potatoes, and carrots.
3. Return chicken to the pan. Add mushrooms and cream/milk.
4. Simmer on low heat until sauce thickens and veggies are tender.

## additional notes / alternative ingredients
* **Alt:** If baking with a crust, use two sheets of store-bought puff pastry for a larger pan.

### **4. Korean Bibimbap (Pork Kasim)**
*Yield: 5 Servings | Prep time: 20 mins | Cook time: 15 mins*

## ingredients
* 750g Pork Kasim (thin strips)
* 2 bunches Blanched pechay or spinach
* 3 large Carrots (julienned) and 250g bean sprouts
* 5 Fried eggs (sunny side up)
* **Sauce:** 5 tbsp Gochujang, 2 tbsp sesame oil, 2 tbsp sugar, 2 tbsp water.

## instructions
1. Saute pork strips in a bit of oil until browned; season with soy sauce.
2. Briefly stir-fry carrots and bean sprouts separately.
3. Assemble: Large bowl of rice, arrange pork and veggies in sections on top.
4. Place an egg on each bowl and drizzle with the Gochujang sauce.

## additional notes / alternative ingredients
* **Tip:** Double the "Magic Sauce" recipe if the family likes it spicy/saucy.

### **5. Classic Pork/Chicken Adobo**
*Yield: 5-6 Servings | Prep time: 10 mins | Cook time: 50 mins*

## ingredients
* 1 kg Meat (Pork chop/Kasim or Chicken)
* 3/4 cup Soy sauce
* 1/2 cup Vinegar
* 1.5 heads Garlic (crushed)
* 3 Bay leaves & 1.5 tsp Peppercorns

## instructions
1. Combine meat, soy sauce, garlic, bay leaves, and peppercorns in a pot. Add 1 cup water.
2. Simmer until meat is tender.
3. Pour in vinegar. **Do not stir** for 5-7 minutes.
4. Reduce the sauce until it reaches your preferred level of "dryness."

## additional notes / alternative ingredients
* **Alt:** Use a mix of pork belly and chicken for "Adobong Ilocano" style.

### **6. Sauteed Talong with Ground Pork**
*Yield: 5 Servings | Prep time: 10 mins | Cook time: 15 mins*

## ingredients
* 5-6 Eggplants (sliced into rounds)
* 500g Ground pork
* 4 tbsp Oyster sauce
* Garlic and 1 large onion

## instructions
1. Fry eggplant slices until soft; set aside.
2. In the same pan, brown the 500g ground pork. Add garlic and onions.
3. Stir in oyster sauce and a splash of water (about 1/4 cup).
4. Toss the fried eggplant back in and mix gently to coat.

## additional notes / alternative ingredients
* **Tip:** Serve with extra steamed rice as the oyster sauce is quite savory.

### **7. Instant Pot Munggo**
*Yield: 5-6 Servings | Prep time: 5 mins | Cook time: 35 mins*

## ingredients
* 1.5 cups Dry mung beans (no soak)
* 400g Pork cubes or 2 pieces tinapa
* 2 chicken cubes
* Garlic, onion, ginger
* 2 cups Malunggay or Alugbati leaves

## instructions
1. Saute pork, garlic, onions, and ginger in the IP.
2. Add dry beans and 6-7 cups of water.
3. Close lid. Set to **Manual High Pressure for 30 mins**.
4. Natural release. Stir in the green leaves using residual heat.

## additional notes / alternative ingredients
* **Sub:** Add sliced bitter melon (ampalaya) if you want that traditional bitterness.

### **8. Beef Nilaga (Instant Pot)**
*Yield: 5 Servings | Prep time: 15 mins | Cook time: 45 mins*

## ingredients
* 1 kg Beef brisket or shank
* 3 Potatoes (quartered)
* 1 large Cabbage or 2 bunches Pechay
* 1 large Onion (quartered)
* 1.5 tbsp Peppercorns & Fish sauce (patis)

## instructions
1. Place beef, onion, peppercorns, and 6 cups water in the IP.
2. Set to **Manual High Pressure for 40 mins**.
3. Quick release. Add potatoes; set to Saute mode for 7 mins.
4. Add cabbage/pechay in the last 2 minutes. Season with patis to taste.

## additional notes / alternative ingredients
* **Alt:** Add 2 corn-on-the-cob (cut into thirds) for more volume.

### **9. Pinoy Bistek**
*Yield: 5 Servings | Prep time: 30 mins | Cook time: 20 mins*

## ingredients
* 800g - 1kg Beef sirloin (thinly sliced)
* 1/2 cup Soy sauce
* 5 tbsp Calamansi juice
* 3 large White onions (sliced into rings)
* Garlic (minced)

## instructions
1. Marinate beef in soy sauce, calamansi, and garlic for at least 30 mins.
2. Pan-fry beef slices quickly in batches. Set aside.
3. Saute half the onion rings until soft.
4. Pour in the marinade; simmer for 3 mins. Return beef to the pan.
5. Top with the remaining raw onion rings and serve immediately.

## additional notes / alternative ingredients
* **Tip:** Don't overcook the beef during the first fry or it will become "makunat" (rubbery).
