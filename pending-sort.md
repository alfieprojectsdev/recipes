### **1\. Lugaw (Instant Pot / Stovetop)**

*Yield: 4 Servings | Prep time: 10 mins | Cook time: 25 mins*

## ingredients

- 2 cups rice (Jasmine or Glutinous)
- Chicken quarters (rubbed with ginger/salt)
- 1 chicken bouillon cube
- 2-inch ginger (sliced into strips)
- 1 head garlic (minced/fried)
- Water (to "Max" line for IP, or 1:7 ratio for stovetop)

## instructions

1. **IP:** Saute garlic, ginger, and chicken. Add rice and water. Set to High Pressure for 20 mins. Natural release.
2. **Stovetop:** Boil chicken and ginger until tender. Add rice; stir frequently until grains bloom and texture is thick.
3. Season with salt or fish sauce to taste.

## additional notes / alternative ingredients

- **Toppings:** Hard-boiled eggs, calamansi, and extra fried garlic.
- **Sub:** Use a mix of 1.5 cups regular rice and 0.5 cups malagkit for a stickier consistency.

### **2\. Max’s Style Fried Chicken**

*Yield: 4 Servings | Prep time: 60 mins | Cook time: 60 mins*

## ingredients

- 1 whole chicken (or leg quarters)
- Calamansi juice (1/4 cup)
- 2 tbsp Fish sauce (patis)
- 1 tsp Black pepper
- Oil for deep frying

## instructions

1. Marinate chicken in calamansi, patis, and pepper for at least 1 hour.
2. Steam the chicken for 30–45 minutes until fully cooked.
3. Remove from steamer and let it air-dry completely (surface must be dry for the "crackle").
4. Flash-fry in very hot oil (180°C) until skin is golden brown and crisp.

## additional notes / alternative ingredients

- **Shortcut:** Use a hair dryer or electric fan to speed up the air-drying process after steaming.

### **3\. Chicken Pastel**

*Yield: 4 Servings | Prep time: 15 mins | Cook time: 25 mins*

## ingredients

- 500g Chicken breast (cubed)
- 1 cup heavy cream or evaporated milk
- 1 Potato & 1 Carrot (cubed)
- 1 can Mushrooms (sliced)
- Butter, garlic, and onions
- 1/2 cup Chorizo de Bilbao (optional)

## instructions

1. Sear chicken in butter until white; set aside.
2. Saute garlic, onion, chorizo, potatoes, and carrots.
3. Return chicken to the pan. Add mushrooms and cream.
4. Simmer on low heat until sauce thickens and veggies are tender.

## additional notes / alternative ingredients

- **Alt:** If baking with a crust, use store-bought puff pastry and bake at **200°C** for 15 mins.

### **4\. Korean Bibimbap (Pork Kasim)**

*Yield: 2 Servings | Prep time: 20 mins | Cook time: 15 mins*

## ingredients

- 300g Pork Kasim (thin strips)
- Blanched pechay or spinach
- Sliced carrots and bean sprouts
- Fried egg (sunny side up)
- **Sauce:** 2 tbsp Gochujang, 1 tbsp sesame oil, 1 tbsp sugar, 1 tbsp water.

## instructions

1. Saute pork strips in a bit of oil until browned; season with a little soy sauce.
2. Briefly stir-fry carrots and bean sprouts separately.
3. Assemble: Rice at the bottom, arrange pork and veggies in sections on top.
4. Place the egg in the center and drizzle with the Gochujang sauce.

## additional notes / alternative ingredients

- **Sub:** Use Mu Saengchae (pickled radish) if available for better acidity.

### **5\. Classic Pork/Chicken Adobo**

*Yield: 4 Servings | Prep time: 10 mins | Cook time: 45 mins*

## ingredients

- 500g Meat (Pork chop/Kasim or Chicken)
- 1/2 cup Soy sauce
- 1/3 cup Vinegar (cane or coconut)
- 1 whole head Garlic (crushed)
- 2 Bay leaves & 1 tsp Peppercorns

## instructions

1. Combine meat, soy sauce, garlic, bay leaves, and peppercorns in a pot. Add 1/2 cup water.
2. Simmer until meat is tender.
3. Pour in vinegar. **Do not stir** for 5 minutes to let the acidity cook off.
4. Reduce the sauce until it reaches your desired consistency.

## additional notes / alternative ingredients

- **Alt:** For "Adobo sa Gata," add 1/2 cup coconut milk in the final 10 minutes of simmering.

### **6\. Sauteed Talong with Ground Pork**

*Yield: 3 Servings | Prep time: 10 mins | Cook time: 15 mins*

## ingredients

- 3 Eggplants (sliced into rounds or strips)
- 250g Ground pork
- 2 tbsp Oyster sauce
- Garlic and onions

## instructions

1. Fry eggplant slices until soft; set aside.
2. In the same pan, brown the ground pork. Add garlic and onions.
3. Stir in oyster sauce and a splash of water.
4. Toss the fried eggplant back in and mix gently to coat.

## additional notes / alternative ingredients

- **Alt:** Add a pinch of chili flakes for a spicy kick.

### **7\. Instant Pot Munggo**

*Yield: 4 Servings | Prep time: 5 mins | Cook time: 35 mins*

## ingredients

- 1 cup Dry mung beans (no soak)
- 200g Pork cubes or tinapa flakes
- 1 chicken cube
- Garlic, onion, ginger
- Malunggay or Alugbati leaves

## instructions

1. Saute pork/tinapa, garlic, onions, and ginger in the IP.
2. Add dry beans and 4–5 cups of water.
3. Close lid. Set to **Manual High Pressure for 25–30 mins**.
4. Natural release. Stir in the green leaves using residual heat before serving.

## additional notes / alternative ingredients

- **Sub:** If no pork/tinapa, use chicharon as a topping for texture.

### **8\. Beef Nilaga (Instant Pot)**

*Yield: 4 Servings | Prep time: 15 mins | Cook time: 40 mins*

## ingredients

- 500g Beef brisket or shank
- 2 Potatoes (quartered)
- 1 head Cabbage or Pechay
- 1 Onion (quartered)
- 1 tbsp Peppercorns & Fish sauce (patis)

## instructions

1. Place beef, onion, peppercorns, and 4 cups water in the IP.
2. Set to **Manual High Pressure for 35 mins**.
3. Quick release. Add potatoes and set to Saute mode for 5–7 mins.
4. Add cabbage/pechay in the last 2 minutes. Season with patis.

## additional notes / alternative ingredients

- **Alt:** Add saba banana (plantain) for a traditional sweet-savory balance.

### **9\. Pinoy Bistek**

*Yield: 4 Servings | Prep time: 30 mins | Cook time: 20 mins*

## ingredients

- 500g Beef sirloin (thinly sliced)
- 1/4 cup Soy sauce
- 3 tbsp Calamansi juice
- 2 large White onions (sliced into rings)
- Garlic (minced)

## instructions

1. Marinate beef in soy sauce, calamansi, and garlic for 30 mins.
2. Pan-fry beef slices quickly (don't overcook). Set aside.
3. In the same pan, saute half the onion rings until translucent.
4. Pour in the marinade and simmer for 2 mins. Return beef to the pan.
5. Top with the remaining raw onion rings and serve.

## additional notes / alternative ingredients

- **Sub:** If calamansi is unavailable, use lemon juice but reduce the quantity slightly.
